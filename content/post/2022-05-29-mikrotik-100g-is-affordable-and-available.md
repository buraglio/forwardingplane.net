So, you need a 100G router or switch, but are deeply concerned about: 

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->

- Cost- Power draw- Cooling issues- Price- Capabilities- Money- Availability- CapEX

<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

The ever-increasing need for faster interfaces, and standardization in the WAN at 10G and 100G rather than 40G or 25G as an interface spec has dramatically increased the need for 100G hardware. With power consumption of most 100G platforms rising to ludicrous levels, and the accompanying cooling required to keep those platforms running smoothly keeping pace with the power draw, one may start to believe there is little hope for 100G for the small to medium size networks. Couple that with supply chain issues and feature requirements, and all may seem fairly bleak.
